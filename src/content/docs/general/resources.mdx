Below you'll find the best resources that will help you become a **psychologist**.

## Websites and articles
- [PsyPost](https://www.psypost.org/) - PsyPost.org is dedicated to reporting the latest peer-reviewed research on human behavior, cognition, and society. They strive to translate complex research into articles that are accessible to a broad audience. This site can keep you informed about the latest developments in psychology and neuroscience, making it an essential resource for students, professionals, and anyone interested in understanding the human mind.
- [Psychcentral](https://psychcentral.com/) - offers a wealth of information on mental health issues, providing resources that range from detailed articles on psychological conditions to tips for everyday mental wellness. Its user-friendly interface makes it easy to navigate through a wide range of topics.
- [VeryWellMind](https://www.verywellmind.com/) - provides clear, expert-reviewed content on mental health and wellness. Its strength lies in offering practical tips and scientifically-backed advice for maintaining mental well-being. The website offers expert-reviewed content that addresses mental health, wellness, and the journey towards a healthier mind.
- [Scientific American Mind & Brain](https://www.scientificamerican.com/mind-and-brain/) - an offshoot of Scientific American, focuses on neuroscience and psychology. It delves into how discoveries in these fields impact our understanding of human behavior and cognition.
- [BPS Research Digest](https://www.bps.org.uk/collections/research-digest) - Published by the British Psychological Society, this digest offers a concise overview of the latest research in psychology.
- [SimplyPsychology](SimplyPsychology) - SimplyPsychology is an educational resource ideal for students and educators. It provides clear, comprehensive overviews of key theories and concepts in psychology.
